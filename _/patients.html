Preparations Before Breath Collections

If you are not able to comply with these guidelines for testing, then you may not be a candidate for a breath test and your medical professional can assist you in determining if another test may accommodate your needs. AerodiagnosticsTM is unable to provide medical advice.

The hydrogen and methane breath collection guidelines below are to be followed for all of our hydrogen and methane breath tests.

  1. No smoking, (including second-hand smoke) for at least one (1) hour before or at any time during the breath collection.
  2. No sleeping or vigorous exercise for at least one (1) hour before or at any time during the breath collection.
  3. If you recently had antibiotic therapy, very heavy diarrhea (which may be caused by other symptoms such as a viral infection or the flu), colonoscopy, barium studies or enemas, please wait 14 days (or as directed by your clinician) after completion of the above mentioned prior to starting your breath test.
  4. All of our hydrogen and methane breath tests require a 24 hour preparation. The first 12 hours prior to your breath collection, you must limit your diet. Foods that you CAN eat during the first 12 hours of your preparation include baked or broiled chicken, fish or turkey (salt and pepper only), white bread (only), plain white potatoes, plain steamed white rice, eggs, clear chicken or beef broth (no vegetables pieces). The second 12 hours prior to collecting your breath you will fast. Do not eat or drink anything other than water.

Can I Collect More Than One Sample On The Same Day?

  • AerodiagnosticsTM offers five different hydrogen and methane breath tests for gastrointestinal disorders (Sucrose, Lactose, Fructose Intolerances/malabsorptions & Small Intestinal Bacterial Overgrowth with Lactulose or Glucose).
  • To ensure accuracy of the results it is not possible to collect breath specimens for more than one breath test on the same day.
  • The highly specific nature of the technology requires a 24 hour preparation. The first 12 hours you will follow a limited diet and you will fast for the second 12 hours prior to collecting your breath specimens.

Insurance Coverage for Breath Tests Ordered by a Licensed Health care Provider

Patients should contact their insurance provider in advance of the test to fully understand their responsibility for any deductible charge, coinsurance, or co-pay that may be required by their insurance carrier as a result of their plan.

Health Insurance Pre-Authorizations

  • A health insurance pre-authorization may or may not be required depending on your specific health insurance carrier and chosen health plan. Health plan insurance products vary as to their requirements regarding pre-authorization. AerodiagnosticsTM recommends that you contact your health insurance organization to learn about any pre-authorization requirements prior to taking a breath test. Many health plans have in and out of network requirements that you should be aware of prior to taking a breath test. If your heath plan provides you with a pre-authorization number, please ensure that number is placed on the AerodiagnosticsTM Requisition along with all pertinent patient and health plan information. Complete and accurate patient and health plan information is the best way to ensure you maximize your allowable benefits under your plan and reduce any unnecessary out of pocket expenses.
